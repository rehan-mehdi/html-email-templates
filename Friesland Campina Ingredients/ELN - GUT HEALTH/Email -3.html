The magic of the microbiome: what aspects of infant health can the gut actually support?
Like us, you’ve probably noticed a lot more talk recently about the human microbiome, or perhaps the gut microbiota, in particular. There’s plenty of interest and excitement brewing as sustained scientific work – within academia and business – uncovers fascinating facts about the gut, how to nurture it and the benefits of doing so.

Emerging science is revealing the role played by the infant's gut microbiota across four key health benefit areas:
  • Digestive health
  • Immunity
  • Cognitive function
  • Growth & development
These benefits are connected with the presence of certain strains of bacteria in the gut, which can be encouraged to grow with the help of the right nutrition: it all comes down to the power of oligosaccharides. These are found in human breast milk as HMOs (human milk oligosaccharides) and can also be derived from cow’s milk as an ingredient known as GOS, or galacto-oligosaccharides.

They are complex and diverse structures which have been shown to offer different health benefits, and thereby complement each other in supporting infant health. For instance, combining GOS with different HMOs like 2’-FL, 3’-SL and LNT makes for a more functionally complete infant milk formula that has the potential to positively impact multiple areas of infant health – for one gut, and for a lifetime of opportunity.
