Dear colleagues,
I hope many of you enjoyed the long sunny days of summer and the chance to spend precious time with your loved ones at home or away – but definitely well away from your computer.

We all need the time to recharge our batteries. The first six months as FC-P have meant a heavy workload all-round, and many struggles despite the strong progress we continue to make. I want to say a big ‘thank you’ to you all for the tremendous work you are doing as we build and come to own our new ‘Professional’ world. Stay true to yourselves and let’s look forward to more non-digital interaction with each other and our customers, and the sunshine behind the clouds!

As well as driving forward our transformation programme and presenting ‘Our Purpose, Our Plan’ to you in the coming months, we have an exciting year of festivities to look forward to as Royal FrieslandCampina celebrates its 150th anniversary as a dairy cooperative. Back in 1871, farmers began to realise that they would be stronger if they stood together – an ethos that we firmly believe in to this day. Events will kick off on Wednesday, 8 September, and that’s just the start. So get ready to mark this very special year in our continuing history of successful partnerships.
Double celebrations
It was double celebrations in China on 31 August. The Debic brand celebrated its 101ST anniversary, and the Chinese team opened the new Enabling Centre. More than 30 national key accounts were invited to join the offline celebrations where the team presented their vision for the Chinese market and discussed future plans. Other customers were invited to join the online celebrations.

The Debic anniversary event was broadcast live with a media partner called Yishalika. There was an online demo by Yewei, a Chinese bakery Master and key opinion leader, attracting over 13,000 page views and leading to more than 332 sales leads.

An icy innovation
If you enjoyed an ice cream or two over the summer holiday, you’ll be all too familiar with the issue of rapid melting. We recently supported our key Chinese customer Daka with our Kievit® Vana-Cerea X832 creamer which we launched in May.
Thanks to great collaboration within the project team, with procurement, and with the Salatiga production location in Indonesia, we were able to speed up the process and launch two months ahead of schedule. As a result, we were ready for the seasonal peak and achieved a total of 1.9k MT this year, with a 7.1k MT volume forecast for next year.
Transformation Team time!
Our primary processes, the focus since FrieslandCampina Professional began in April, are now well under control. To drive the next step, we have established a Transformation Team comprising Felix Werdmüller (Sales), Benedikt Wichmann (Finance), Marieke Tromp & Kim Brom (Customer SC) and team leader Annemiek Boogaarts. This team will help the teams to simplify our ‘Professional’ end-to-end way of working.

In the last quarter, we changed the way we report financially and are now able to better track customer/material profitability insights. We have also focused on what activities can best be run by which team. From 1 September, we will start by integrating the Planning of Butter team into our Customer SC.

We are banking on your ideas to reduce workload by focusing on value adding activities, so please keep your creative juices flowing!
News from you …
In quarter 2 of 2021, we asked for your opinions about how we’re delivering for you as FrieslandCampina Professional.

75% of you responded to our O2Y (Over to You) survey. We’re delighted to report that:
  • There is a great sense of belonging
  • We comply excellently with business codes of conduct and ethics
  • You feel treated with respect and dignity (and we are absolutely here for you if that’s not how you are feeling!)
We also learned these valuable lessons:
  • We need to remove obstacles to allow you to work at your best (we are keen to do that for you, please share your feedback with your manager)
  • We have the data and details to make the right decisions, at the right time, at the right level – we need to capitalise on that
  • We can do more to make you all feel more connected, across countries, across departments and across our core business – finding the best solutions for our customers
