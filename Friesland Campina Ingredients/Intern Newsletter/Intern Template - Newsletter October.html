Dear colleagues,
At the end of our first six months, we can look back with pride on what we have achieved so far. In this issue of the newsletter, we can again report numerous successes which we as the Professional team achieved over the last weeks!

As we enter the final quarter of the year, we will continue to pull together to pursue our big FrieslandCampina Professional dream. We have an exciting internal event ahead of us: put 16 November in your diaries and tune in as we will further detail out ‘Our Purpose, Our Plan’ (OPOP), our enablers and our FC-P brand and everyone’s contribution to those topics.

We thank you all for your ongoing efforts, and don’t forget to keep your feedback coming. We need to know what’s working for you, and where we can make processes even more efficient.
Lattiz strengthens relationships
Lattiz reconnected with its French partners during our very own ‘Lattiz tour de France’. Pierre Leonard and Abbès Amara of Lattiz France are visiting 15 partner depots over a 7-week period to maintain the strong relationships necessary to fuel growth in this important market.
The Kiosk Koffie Cup 2021
On 15 September, Lattiz and coffee roaster partner Smit & Dorlas hosted the first live event in the Netherlands since the lockdown in the form of a coffee championship for one of our biggest customers, Kiosk. Kiosk is responsible for supplying food and drinks at 111 outlets across the Dutch rail network.
This event, which was held at one of our farmer’s locations in Boxtel, was a huge success! Kiosk was very happy and we were able to improve the quality of the coffee served at Dutch railway stations!
90th anniversary in Japan
We recently celebrated a remarkable achievement: our 90th anniversary with our partner Nosawa in Japan. Nosawa began importing cheese from FrieslandCampina back in 1931. We now sell a broad portfolio to Nosawa, including branded cheese (Frico) and industrial cheeses.
To mark this special occasion, we had a Frico cheese wheel custom-made and couriered over to Japan, since we were sadly unable to celebrate this achievement in person.
Hospitality hots up
Our events team visited the ‘Gastvrij Rotterdam’ hospitality fair which took place in the Netherlands from 20 to 22 September 2021. The aim was to get a feeling for how events are being organised now that society is opening up again.
  • More than 300 exhibitors participated
  • 11,264 visitors visited the first Dutch food fair since the COVID pandemic began (17% down on 2019)
  • There was a strict entrance policy, but everything felt normal during the fair itself
  • Attendees were able to eat, drink and network as usual, and face masks were not mandatory.
We will participate at the ‘BBB’ hospitality event in Maastricht from 11 to 13 October with a small promotional point as an initial pilot in the Netherlands.
On Trend Specials 2022
The latest Trend Report for industrial food customers will be unveiled during a webinar on 14 October. This latest report is packed with hip and happening trends and the freshest insights.

New: this year we have included growth hacking data as well as a broader range of products, including Cheese and Butter & Milkpowder.
The campaign went live on 14 September and will run until April 2022. We have seen a strong response so far to the invitation to register for the webinar, and we will keep you posted on the overall response to the launch.
Winning innovations
For project Mario Kart, our Technical Sales team conducted a market validation experiment for independent pizzerias. They visited pizza operators in Germany and learned more about their cheese requirements for pizza applications.

And our Technical Sales specialist for beverages visited our customer Prosol in Spain to introduce, explain and demonstrate our new Kievit® Vana-Cappa X01 foam booster. They will be moving on towards recipe development and testing
Grand Opening of the Inspiration Café
Today marks the eagerly awaited opening of our Inspiration Café - our new meeting space in Wageningen (NL). Margrethe and Roger cut the ribbon. The Inspiration Café will allow customers to taste our ingredients in a setting that replicates the end consumer experience. The space is equipped with full broadcast capabilities for hosting remote demos too. Let’s all embrace the possibility to meet up again in person and take full advantage of this unique experience!
Over to You feedback
Over to You (O2U) has provided some valuable insights into the obstacles people in our community face in doing their jobs. The workload and pressure are too great; where they exist at all, processes are not always clear; and tools and systems are not working effectively. These are areas we will be tackling. We are already working to optimise certain areas, such as customer complaints handling.

The Transformation Team is always keen to receive your input about what’s working well, as well as understanding where there is scope for improvement. Please continue to keep your line manager informed of all your positives and negatives. It’s just as important that we know what is working well and what we need to keep doing. It’s only through open and honest communication that we can implement change for the better.
